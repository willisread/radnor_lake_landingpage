Radnor Lake State Park

Radnor Lake is wonderful getaway from the city, located just 20 minutes from downtown Nashville. Park visitors can enjoy hiking the miles of unpaved trails, observing the various types of fauna and flora or just spending time away from the hustle and bustle of the city for a few hours.

Find out more

Tennessee wildlife up close and personal

There are wide array of animals that can be seen by visitors throughout the year, no matter the season. Owls, herons and water fowl as well as many species of amphibians, reptiles and mammals such as mink and otter.

Hit the Trails

Radnor Lake offers more than 6 miles of hiking trails for visitors. We have various kinds of trails based on experience level.

  1. Ganier Range Trail (1.65 miles)
  2. Lake Trail (1.35 mi)
  3. South Cove Trail (1.40 mi)
  4. South Lake Trail (0.90 mi)
  5. Spillway Trail (0.25 mi)

Walter Criley Visitor Center

First time visitors to Radnor Lake State Park are encourged to stop by the visitor center to get acquainted with the park. Here are a few of the highlights:

  • Cultural/Historical Artifacts
  • Wildlife Displays
  • Film-Conservation of Radnor Lake in the 1970s
  • Wall-size Map of Trail System
